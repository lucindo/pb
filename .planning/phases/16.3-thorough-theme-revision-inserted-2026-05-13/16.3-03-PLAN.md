Dark-specific rubric notes (RESEARCH §Pattern 2 adapted for Dark):
  - `bg` = source's `base` (e.g. Frappé `#303446`, Mocha `#1e1e2e`, Nord nord0 `#2e3440`, Tokyo Night Storm `#1a1b26`)
  - `bg-soft` = source's `mantle` (one tier DEEPER for Dark — i.e. card-bg-soft is darker than page-bg for "card sits LOWER" feel? Actually rubric inverts here: in Dark palettes the card surface is typically LIGHTER than page bg, e.g. Frappé surface0 `#414559` > base `#303446`). Operator/agent should follow source's intended "card lift" convention.
  - `bg-edge` = source's `crust` (deepest — typically lowest tier)
  - `surface` = source's `surface0` or `surface1` (lifted inner surface)
  - `accent-strong` = source's deeper saturated accent (mauve, blue-bright, or a deeper-tier color). MUST clear D-01 vs on-accent.
  - `on-accent` = source's `base` or `crust` (dark text on lighter accent-strong)
  - `orb-in-from/-to` = source's LIGHT-POLE blue/lavender family (sky/blue/lavender). EVEN IN DARK, In must be the LIGHTER pole (RESEARCH Pitfall 1 — easy to invert here).
  - `orb-out-from/-to` = source's DARK-POLE neutral family (surface1/surface2 OR a deep saturated accent like Frappé's `surface1` #51576d). Out is the DARKER pole.
  - `orb-in-text` = source's deepest available text (crust or base) — dark text on the lighter In gradient
  - `orb-out-text` = source's lightest text (text or subtext0) — light text on the darker Out gradient

Polarity inversion warning (RESEARCH Pitfall 1): Dark sources frequently have semantic "primary" hues at LOWER luminance than warm accents. A naive `source.blue → orb-in-from` AND `source.peach → orb-out-from` mapping for Frappé would produce inMidLum < outMidLum because Frappé's `blue` (#8caaee, lum ≈ 0.41) is DARKER than `peach` (#ef9f76, lum ≈ 0.50). The rubric MUST verify polarity and route differently — use source's `lavender` + `blue` for the In (lighter than `peach`), and use source's `surface1` + `surface0` for the Out (darker than `lavender`+`blue`). The 16.3-01 Worked Example demonstrates this routing.
