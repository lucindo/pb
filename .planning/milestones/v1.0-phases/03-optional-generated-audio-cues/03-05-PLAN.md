<objective>
Phase 3 has two qualities that NO automated test can verify:
1. **Subjective audio quality** — does the bowl cue feel calm? Is In perceptibly higher than Out? Is the mute fade-out smooth? Is the 3-2-1 tick distinct from the gong?
2. **Real-device platform behavior** — iOS Safari hardware-mute switch routing, AudioContext interrupted-state on phone calls, background-tab continuation.

This plan is a sequence of human-verification checkpoints that cover both. The plan is non-autonomous: each checkpoint pauses for user approval, and the optional bowl-synthesis tuning checkpoints (1, 2, 3) may produce edits to `src/audio/cueSynth.ts`, `src/audio/cueSynth.test.ts`, `src/audio/audioEngine.ts`, and/or `src/audio/audioEngine.test.ts` based on the user's listening preference. Per checker I2, the `files_modified` frontmatter declares all four files so that subsequent gates (and any cross-file consistency checks) know that test files travel with their source files when constants are retuned.

Purpose: Close the "automated tests pass but real users hate the sound" gap. AUDI-01 and AUDI-02 are not "done" until a human has used the app and signed off.

Output: Possibly-modified cueSynth.ts and/or audioEngine.ts (frequency / decay / peakGain / fade-time tuning), the matching test files updated to keep assertions in lock-step, and a SUMMARY.md documenting the UAT checklist results.
</objective>
